Stride Labs — Sizing & Fit Guide

Stride Labs shoes run true to size for most runners, but running shoes are
usually worn a half size larger than your everyday shoes to allow the foot to
swell over distance.

How to measure your size
- Measure both feet in the late afternoon, when feet are at their largest.
- Stand on a sheet of paper, mark heel to longest toe, and measure in cm.
- Use the longer of the two feet, then check the size chart on the product page.
- Leave about a thumb's width (1–1.5 cm) between your longest toe and the end
  of the shoe.

Width options
- Standard width fits most runners.
- Wide width adds room across the forefoot and is recommended if your feet feel
  pinched in the standard fit, or if you have bunions or wide toes.

Pronation and support
- Neutral runners suit the Tempo, Pulse, Aero and Terra as designed.
- If you overpronate (arches collapse inward), add a Stride Stability insole,
  sold separately, for extra medial support. Stride Labs shoes are neutral by
  design; we do not currently make a dedicated motion-control shoe.

Between sizes?
- For daily trainers (Tempo, Terra) size up for comfort.
- For race shoes (Pulse, Aero) size down for a snug, locked-in fit.

If your first pair does not fit, the Stride Labs return and exchange policy lets
you swap sizes free of charge within the trial window (see Returns & Exchanges).
