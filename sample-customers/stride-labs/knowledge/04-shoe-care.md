Stride Labs — Shoe Care & Durability

How long should a pair last?
- Road shoes (Tempo, Pulse) typically last 600–800 km before the midsole foam
  loses its cushioning and rebound.
- The Aero carbon racer is a race-day shoe: expect 250–350 km of peak
  performance. It still runs after that, just with less "pop".
- The Terra trail shoe lasts 500–700 km depending on terrain; rocky and muddy
  routes wear the outsole faster.

Signs it is time to replace
- New aches in the knees, hips or shins after previously comfortable runs.
- Visibly compressed or wrinkled midsole foam.
- Worn-through outsole rubber exposing the midsole.
- The shoe feels "flat" and unresponsive compared to when it was new.

Cleaning
- Remove the insoles and laces, then brush off dirt with a soft brush.
- Hand-wash the upper with cool water and mild soap. Do NOT machine wash or
  tumble dry — heat breaks down the midsole and adhesives.
- Air-dry away from direct heat. Stuff with paper towel to hold the shape and
  speed drying.

Make them last longer
- Rotate two pairs if you run often; foam recovers between runs, extending life.
- Keep road shoes off technical trails, and untie laces before taking shoes off
  to protect the heel counter.
- Store at room temperature, out of direct sun, never in a hot car.
